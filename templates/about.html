About the ELO Rating System

The ELO Rating System is a method for calculating the relative skill levels of players in zero-sum games such as chess. It is named after its creator Arpad Elo, a Hungarian-American physics professor.

How it works

The ELO system calculates an expected score for each player based on their current rating. After a game, the actual result is compared with the expected result, and the rating is adjusted accordingly. Players gain more points for beating higher-rated opponents and lose more points when defeated by lower-rated opponents.

Our Implementation

In our system, all players start with a rating of 1500. The rating changes after each confirmed game, with the amount of change depending on:

  • The difference between the players' ratings
  • The score of the game
  • A K-factor that determines the maximum possible adjustment

Reporting Games

To ensure fair play, all game results must be reported by one player and confirmed by the opponent. This prevents incorrect or fraudulent reports from affecting ratings.

How to use the system

To use the system, you need to register via Telegram bot @nu_tt_alpha_bot. Then you can report your games and see the ratings of other players. Then follow the instructions in the bot.
